\chapter{CAGR Calculation and Interpretation}
\label{sec:appendix_cagr}

The Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) is widely used to evaluate the average annual growth rate of a value over a period of time, assuming compounding.

\[
\text{CAGR} = \left( \frac{V_f}{V_i} \right)^{\frac{1}{n}} - 1
\]

\noindent
Where:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \( V_f \): Final value
  \item \( V_i \): Initial value
  \item \( n \): Number of years
\end{itemize}

\noindent
Example: From 2020 to 2024, the value increased from 3 to 16.6. The CAGR is:

\[
\left( \frac{16.6}{3} \right)^{\frac{1}{4}} - 1 \approx 0.534 \text{ or } 53.4\%
\]

This metric is used in Chapter~\ref{introduction} to analyze EV market trends.
